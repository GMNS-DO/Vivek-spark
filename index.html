💡 Tips to Move Forward
1
Have the Career Conversation Early
Don't wait for your annual review to talk about where you want to go. Schedule a dedicated 30 minutes with your manager this month — just about your future, not your current tasks.
2
Build Visibility, Not Just Skills
Skills get you ready. Visibility gets you considered. Volunteer for cross-functional projects, present your work, and let the right people know what you're capable of.
3
Update Your Internal Profile
Your internal profile is often the first thing a hiring manager sees. Make sure it reflects your current experience, aspirations, and achievements — not just your job title.

✅ Career Readiness Checklist
I know what my next career step looks like
You have a clear direction — even if broad — and you've shared it with your manager.
I've identified 1–2 skills to build this quarter
Focused development beats scattered learning. Pick the skills that move the needle most for your goal.
I have a mentor or growth conversation scheduled
Learning from someone who's been where you want to go accelerates everything.
My work is visible beyond my immediate team
Have you contributed to something cross-functional or presented your work to a wider audience recently?
